# FARMCHAIN
*Blockchain-Enabled Agricultural Supply Chain & Accountability Network*

**Project Blueprint — Version 1.0**

---

## PROJECT ABSTRACT

FarmChain is a blockchain-anchored, end-to-end agricultural supply chain platform that tracks produce from farm registration through consumer purchase. By combining real-time freshness scoring (FRS), weight-based tamper detection, AI-assisted visual estimation, blockchain-immutable audit trails, community funding mechanisms, and transparent subsidy disbursement, FarmChain creates a trusted, accountable, and incentive-aligned network for every participant from smallholder farmer to end consumer.

---

## SECTION 1 — VISION, GOALS & PROBLEM STATEMENT

### 1. Vision & Problem Statement

#### 1.1 The Core Problem

Agricultural supply chains in India are long, fragmented, and opaque. Produce passes through multiple intermediaries — farmers, aggregators, transporters, middlemen (commission agents), and retailers — with no unified accountability layer. This creates several chronic failures:

- Quality degradation that cannot be traced to a responsible party
- Price manipulation by middlemen who control quantity information
- Artificial preservative use to fake freshness and extend shelf life
- Subsidy leakage that never reaches the most vulnerable, low-income farmers
- Consumer mistrust with no mechanism to verify what they are buying
- Farmer exploitation with no protection against rejection of produce without evidence

#### 1.2 The FarmChain Solution

FarmChain addresses each failure through five integrated pillars:

| **Pillar** | **What It Does** | **Who Benefits** |
|---|---|---|
| Traceability | Immutable blockchain log of every custody transfer | All parties |
| FRS Scoring | Weight-based objective freshness grading at every node | Buyers, consumers |
| Accountability | Pinpoints bad actors via on-chain dispute resolution | Farmers, retailers |
| Community Finance | Consumer seed/fertiliser funding with equity contracts | Farmers, investors |
| Subsidy Engine | Prioritised disbursement to low-income registered farmers | Smallholders |

## SECTION 2 — NETWORK PARTICIPANTS & ROLES

### 2. Participants & Their Roles

#### 2.1 Farmers / Producers

The originating node of every supply chain event. Farmers register once on-chain and receive a permanent Farmer ID that is tied to their land parcel, village of origin, income tier classification, and produce categories.

- Register produce batch at harvest with: crop type, weight (W_origin), count (N), GPS location, village, harvest date
- Seal crate with tamper-evident QR tag generated by the app
- Income tier is determined at registration via land holdings, annual income declaration, and cross-verification with government databases — this governs subsidy priority
- Earn indemnity insurance coverage funded by a portion of GAS fees from all transactions in their produce chain
- Receive smart-contract-based payments upon confirmed FRS grading at destination — eliminating middleman payment delays

#### 2.2 Transporters / Transit Nodes

Transporters accept sealed crates and are contractually bound by the blockchain record to deliver them without tampering. Each transit leg is a separate blockchain transaction.

- Scan crate QR at pickup — blockchain records departure timestamp, vehicle ID, transporter wallet
- Crate must remain sealed — broken seal flags an automatic dispute event on-chain
- Optional: IoT temperature and humidity sensors can be linked to a crate's blockchain record for cold-chain produce
- Transporter reputation score is updated after each delivery based on FRS outcome and seal integrity — visible to all network participants

> **FLAG:** *Transporters who consistently deliver produce with broken seals or anomalous FRS drops will accumulate negative reputation and can be flagged for network review. Persistent bad actors can be blacklisted via a governance vote.*

#### 2.3 Middlemen / Commission Agents

Middlemen (MMs) aggregate produce from multiple farmers and redistribute to multiple retailers. In the current system they are the least accountable node. FarmChain transforms them into a transparent, rated intermediary.

- Each MM must register and receive an MM ID — they cannot operate on the network anonymously
- All batches they receive and re-dispatch are logged; they cannot hide quantity splits or re-aggregations
- Retailers rate the MM and their batch consistency after each transaction — the MM Review & Quality Assurance score (MRQA) is publicly visible
- Attempting to underreport quantity or commingle batches of different origins is detectable via blockchain weight and count records
- Market manipulation of apparent scarcity is prevented: the blockchain database shows real-time network-wide quantity of any produce type — regulators and retailers can query this

#### 2.4 Retailers / Shopkeepers

The final commercial node before consumer purchase. Retailers bear the responsibility of confirming delivery FRS and providing honest batch feedback.

- Weigh arriving crate, calculate FRS using the app, record W_dest and grade on-chain
- Rate the MM on: delivery consistency, quantity accuracy, packaging quality, communication
- Flag any broken seal or count discrepancy — this triggers an automated dispute resolution workflow
- Retailers who fail to record FRS (attempt to skip accountability) are flagged — partial payment holds apply

#### 2.5 Consumers

Consumers are the end-demand signal and a powerful accountability actor in FarmChain.

- Scan produce QR in store to see the full chain-of-custody — farm origin, every transit node, all FRS scores
- Report quality issues in-app after purchase — reports are linked back to the specific blockchain batch record
- Consumer reports trigger a weighted dispute score — if multiple consumers flag the same batch/MM/transporter, the issue escalates
- Optionally fund farmer seed and fertiliser via blockchain-based equity contracts (see Section 5)

## SECTION 3 — FRESHNESS SCORING SYSTEM (FRS)

### 3. Fresh Rate Score (FRS) — Core Technical Engine

#### 3.1 The Principle

The FRS is built on a single, scientifically-validated principle: water makes up 80-95% of fresh produce. As produce loses freshness, it loses water through transpiration — and that water loss directly manifests as weight loss. By measuring the weight at origin (W_origin) and at each subsequent checkpoint (W_dest), the system derives an objective, tamper-resistant freshness score without requiring any lab equipment.

#### 3.2 FRS Grading Tables

The formula is universal — what changes by produce type is only the grading threshold applied. Three tiers are defined based on published postharvest research (UC Davis, FAO, AVRDC):

**Standard Produce (Default)**

Applies to: Apples, oranges, mangoes, tomatoes (with calyx), capsicum, brinjal, cucumber, carrots, beans, cauliflower, cabbage, grapes.

| **FRS Range** | **Grade** | **Label** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 98 – 100% | A+ | Premium | Full price — top shelf |
| 95 – 97.9% | A | Fresh | Full price — marketable |
| 90 – 94.9% | B | Acceptable | Slight discount — sell within 2 days |
| 85 – 89.9% | C | Reduced | Significant discount — sell today |
| Below 85% | D | Rejected | Dispute triggered — no full payment |

**High-Sensitivity Produce**

Applies to: Spinach, lettuce, coriander, mint, strawberries, mushrooms, figs, fresh herbs, berries, sprouts.

| **FRS Range** | **Grade** | **Label** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 98 – 100% | A+ | Premium | Full price |
| 96 – 97.9% | A | Fresh | Full price |
| 93 – 95.9% | B | Acceptable | Moderate discount |
| 90 – 92.9% | C | Reduced | Deep discount — sell immediately |
| Below 90% | D | Rejected | Dispute triggered |

**High-Tolerance Produce**

Applies to: Pumpkin, coconut, jackfruit, sweet potato, ginger, garlic, onions, watermelon (whole), plantain, sugarcane.

| **FRS Range** | **Grade** | **Label** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 95 – 100% | A+ | Premium | Full price |
| 92 – 94.9% | A | Fresh | Full price |
| 88 – 91.9% | B | Acceptable | Minor discount |
| 85 – 87.9% | C | Reduced | Sell soon |
| Below 85% | D | Rejected | Dispute triggered |

#### 3.3 FRS at Every Node — Multi-Hop Tracking

FRS is not calculated only at final delivery. It is recorded at every point of custody transfer: farm dispatch, aggregation at the MM, loading at a transit depot, and arrival at the retailer. This multi-hop record enables the system to pinpoint exactly where quality degraded.

> **TRACE:** *Example: If FRS is 97% at farm dispatch, 96% at MM aggregation, 91% at transit depot, and 88% at retailer — the system automatically flags the transit depot-to-retailer leg as the degradation event and initiates a dispute against the transporter responsible for that leg.*

#### 3.4 AI-Assisted Visual Estimation

Alongside weight-based FRS, the mobile app includes an AI scan-and-estimate feature at each node. Participants photograph the opened crate and the system:

- Estimates item count (cross-checks with declared N to detect quantity fraud)
- Detects visible spoilage, bruising, mould, or colour anomalies
- Flags potential artificial preservative use (unusual surface coating, abnormal colour uniformity) for manual review
- Generates a visual freshness confidence score to supplement the weight-based FRS

Visual data is stored as an IPFS hash referenced in the blockchain transaction — it cannot be altered retroactively.

#### 3.5 Forced Expiry & Preservative Flagging

At the point of farm registration, each batch is assigned a Pre-Determined Estimated Expiry (PDEE) — a blockchain-computed expiry window based on produce type, observed FRS at origin, and standard transit duration.

- If a batch arrives at a retailer with an FRS that is inconsistently high relative to its transit time and temperature, the system flags it as a Freshness Anomaly
- Freshness Anomalies trigger a mandatory physical inspection request and can result in a 'Forced Expiry Override' label
- Products exceeding their PDEE window cannot be relisted as fresh — they are automatically marked as discounted or expired in the network

## SECTION 4 — BLOCKCHAIN ARCHITECTURE & ACCOUNTABILITY

### 4. Blockchain Architecture

#### 4.1 Transaction Design

Each physical event in the supply chain maps to a blockchain transaction. The core on-chain record for each crate at each node contains:

| **Field** | **Value Example** | **Recorded By** |
|---|---|---|
| Batch ID | BATCH-KA-2024-00341 | System (auto-generated) |
| Produce Type | Mango — Standard Category | Farmer |
| W_origin | 10.0 kg | Farmer |
| N (count) | 48 items | Farmer |
| GPS Origin | 13.2847°N, 77.1012°E, Tumkur | Auto (GPS) |
| FRS at node | 96.4% | Receiving party |
| Seal status | Intact / Broken | Receiving party |
| Timestamp | 2024-03-15 07:42 IST | System |
| Node wallet | 0xA3F...2B9 | System |
| Visual hash | QmX3T...7Rp (IPFS) | System (AI scan) |

#### 4.2 GAS Fees as the Economic Engine

GAS fees — the small transaction costs inherent to blockchain operations — serve a triple purpose in FarmChain beyond mere transaction processing:

- **Farmer Insurance Pool (PF layer):** A percentage of all GAS fees from transactions involving a farmer's produce flows into a dedicated indemnity pool for that farmer — creating a self-funded insurance mechanism proportional to their network activity
- **Dispute Compensation Fund:** A portion of GAS fees is pooled to fund compensation payouts when bad actors are identified and penalised via the dispute resolution system
- **Network Maintenance & Governance:** The remainder funds platform maintenance, oracle services, and governance token distribution

> **KEY:** *This means every scan, every weigh-in, and every blockchain record actively builds a financial safety net for farmers — without requiring a separate premium payment. Farmers who participate more actively accumulate larger indemnity pools.*

#### 4.3 Dispute Resolution & Bad Actor Identification

When a quality failure is detected (low FRS, broken seal, count discrepancy, consumer report), an automated Dispute Event is created on-chain. The resolution process is:

1. Dispute Event created — all parties in the affected transit leg are notified
2. System auto-analysis — FRS delta between nodes is calculated to narrow the responsible leg
3. Evidence submission window (48 hours) — any party can submit counter-evidence (photos, temperature logs, seal photos)
4. Governance panel review — a rotating panel of verified network participants reviews evidence and votes
5. Verdict and compensation — penalised party's payment is withheld or clawed back; compensation disbursed from the Dispute Fund
6. Reputation score updated on-chain — permanently visible to all future counterparties

#### 4.4 Live Outpost & Depot Monitoring

For major transit depots and aggregation hubs, FarmChain supports a live dashboard mode. Depot operators can view:

- All crates currently in their facility — ripeness status, time since last FRS scan, PDEE countdown
- Alert notifications when any crate is approaching expiry window
- Visual map of in-transit crates across the network with estimated arrival freshness

Trusted digital data is ensured because every data point feeding the dashboard must originate from a verified blockchain transaction — there is no manual override pathway that bypasses the chain.

#### 4.5 Market Manipulation Prevention

A persistent problem in Indian agricultural markets is artificial scarcity — middlemen collectively withholding supply to inflate prices. FarmChain exposes this structurally:

- The blockchain database records the quantity of every registered batch at every node in real time
- Regulators, market committees (APMCs), and large retailers can query: total tomato volume in transit in Karnataka today — making coordinated withholding impossible to conceal
- Anomalies between declared quantities and actual market arrivals trigger automatic regulatory alerts

## SECTION 5 — CONSUMER FUNDING & EQUITY CONTRACTS

### 5. Consumer Seed & Fertiliser Funding (Forced Equity Contracts)

#### 5.1 The Concept

Farmers, especially smallholders, face a chronic pre-season cash crunch for seed and fertiliser purchases. FarmChain enables consumers to directly fund these input purchases in exchange for an equity stake in the harvest — removing exploitative moneylender intermediaries entirely.

#### 5.2 How It Works

- A registered farmer lists a Funding Request on the platform: crop type, land area, input requirements, estimated yield, and proposed equity split
- Consumers browse Funding Requests and contribute any amount — even as small as Rs. 100 — to a farmer's input fund
- A smart contract is created on-chain encoding: contribution amount, equity percentage, harvest season, and payout conditions
- When the harvest is sold through FarmChain, the smart contract automatically calculates each investor's proportional share of proceeds and disburses it to their wallet
- If the harvest fails (due to weather, pest etc.), an insurance payout from the GAS fee pool covers a portion of investor losses — this is the 'forced equity' protection mechanism

#### 5.3 Protections & Governance

- Farmers cannot receive a second funding round until the first season's smart contract is settled — preventing fraud
- All funding contracts are public on-chain — consumers can see how farmers have performed in past seasons before contributing
- A Farmer Reliability Score (based on past yields, FRS performance, and contract fulfilment rate) is displayed on each Funding Request
- Partial crop failure payouts are proportional to FRS data — confirmed poor quality at origin results in proportionally reduced returns, not zero

> **WHY:** *This mechanism also creates powerful consumer alignment with farm-level quality — a consumer who has financially backed a farmer has a direct incentive to buy their produce and give honest quality feedback. It transforms a passive buyer into a supply chain stakeholder.*

## SECTION 6 — SUBSIDY DISBURSEMENT ENGINE

### 6. Intelligent Subsidy Disbursement

#### 6.1 The Problem with Current Subsidies

Government agricultural subsidies in India suffer from three systemic failures: leakage to ineligible recipients, delay in disbursement, and inequitable distribution that favours larger landholders. FarmChain addresses all three.

#### 6.2 The FarmChain Subsidy Engine

The Subsidy Engine operates on a priority-weighted disbursement model with four inputs:

| **Input** | **Description** | **Weight** |
|---|---|---|
| Income Tier | Verified low-income classification at registration | 40% |
| Land Holdings | Smaller land = higher priority score | 25% |
| Network Activity | Active on-chain participants receive faster disbursement | 20% |
| Crop Risk Profile | High-risk crops (perishables, climate-sensitive) get priority | 15% |

#### 6.3 Disbursement Mechanism

- Subsidy funds (from government or NGO partners) are deposited into a verified smart contract pool
- The engine calculates each registered farmer's priority score and queues disbursements accordingly — automatically, without human intermediary intervention
- Farmers receive subsidy directly to their registered wallet — no intermediary can siphon funds
- All disbursements are publicly auditable on-chain — regulators can verify in real time that funds reached their intended recipients
- Farmers with no smartphone access can register through a network of verified Village Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) who facilitate registration and wallet management

#### 6.4 Anti-Gaming Measures

- Income tier declarations are cross-verified against PM-Kisan and state land records databases via API
- Wallets receiving subsidy are flagged if they subsequently attempt large commercial transactions inconsistent with the declared income tier
- Duplicate registrations are detected via Aadhaar-linked identity verification

## SECTION 8 — RISKS & MITIGATIONS

### 8. Risks & Mitigations

| **Risk** | **Impact** | **Mitigation** |
|---|---|---|
| Low smartphone penetration among farmers | High | VLE-assisted registration; feature-phone USSD fallback for basic scans |
| Scale weighing inaccuracy or fraud | High | Certified scale requirement; spot audit programme; AI visual cross-check |
| Middlemen resistance to transparency | High | Incentive: MMs with high MRQA scores get preferential access to premium retailer contracts on-chain |
| Blockchain GAS fee costs at scale | Medium | Layer-2 rollup solution or permissioned blockchain for internal transactions |
| Consumer apathy toward QR scanning | Medium | Gamified loyalty points for scanning; equity contract ROI as strong incentive |
| Government API reliability for subsidy cross-check | Medium | Asynchronous verification with provisional disbursement and retroactive clawback if mismatch |
| Produce category edge cases in FRS | Low | Produce-specific exception rules (banana, tomato, grapes etc.) already encoded per research document |

## APPENDIX — FRS QUICK REFERENCE

### Appendix A: FRS Produce Category Quick Reference

| **Category** | **Produce Examples** | **Table** | **Rejection Threshold** |
|---|---|---|---|
| High-Sensitivity | Spinach, lettuce, coriander, mushrooms, strawberries, berries | Tight | Below 90% FRS |
| Standard | Apples, mangoes, tomatoes (with calyx), carrots, grapes, beans | Default | Below 85% FRS |
| High-Tolerance | Pumpkin, coconut, garlic, onions, ginger, jackfruit, watermelon (whole) | Wide | Below 85% FRS |

### Appendix B: Special Produce Handling Notes

- **Bananas:** Record ripeness stage (green/turning/yellow) at both nodes; green = use High-Tolerance, ripe = use Standard
- **Tomatoes:** Always pack with calyx intact; calyx-removed tomatoes degrade 2-3x faster and are flagged at origin
- **Grapes:** Supplement FRS with a visual stem-condition note (green / partial brown / fully brown)
- **Watermelon/Muskmelon:** Ship whole only — cut melons must be graded as High-Sensitivity
- **Onions/Garlic:** Weigh AFTER curing, never pre-curing; curing weight is the correct W_origin
- **Leafy Greens:** Must be sold same-day if possible; wrap in moist cloth for transit; AI scan flags wilting automatically
- **Mushrooms:** Pack in paper bags (not plastic); generate own heat — depot monitoring mandatory for this category
- **Root Vegetables (Carrots, Radish):** Remove leafy tops before packing and weighing for transit over 2 hours
